As the Spring 2021 semester comes to an end, I would like to build a time machine to go back to the beginning of my software engineering education, but without all the Back to the Future shenanigans. I want to go back and see exactly what I have learned within the past few months on web design and project management. Of course, I am in no means an expert in software development, but this software engineering class has truly altered my views and habits in computer programming. Six months ago I would have never expected myself to reach this far in software engineering, especially since I am not a computer science major. Through my experiences, I have grasped some concepts on coding standards and project management. 

## The Grammar to Programming

I have not always been the best at grammar, especially in my english papers or when I speak. Sometimes I feel bad when I write papers because they will make sense in my head but make no sense to someone else’s. Luckily programming does not involve grammar… or does it? It is extremely important to have a clean and consistent coding style, especially in a team project. Oftentimes, previous codes will be looked at and referenced, so it must be made easy to read. So yes, programming does involve grammar. 

Learning about coding standards and ESLint was one of the most important when dealing with software engineering. Organizing code with proper spaces and brackets makes it easier to separate sections of code for easy navigation. While working on coding assignments and projects, I often find myself referencing my previous codes for guidance. Since I applied proper coding standards to those assignments, it was much easier to navigate and understand. Especially for the final project in my software engineering course, I needed to apply the knowledge I learned throughout the entire semester and reference a lot of previous codes. Additionally, working with a group that also applied proper coding standards made merging files and understanding their part of the code much easier. While it can be annoying to always follow a set of “rules” while coding, it will save time in the long run.

Outside of coding, it is important to consider how other people will interpret your work. Organization and effective documentation are key factors in the engineering field, and my new knowledge of coding standards will aid me in accomplishing those. 

## Planning and Execution

Whenever you need to work in a team setting, the utmost important aspect is effective project management. It is always necessary to document any progress done on a project so other members of the team are informed of what is completed and what needs to be done. Additionally, in order to ensure to utilize time efficiently, tasks for each team member should be designed so that every member is capable of working parallel with each other. 

I usually never find myself committed to plan out and document everything I do. At most, I just set reminders of due dates and that seemed to be working well for me so far. Setting schedules and splitting one task into multiple sub-tasks just seemed unnecessary to me in the past. But to be honest, I have not really dealt with large scaled group projects before that required substantial planning. It was not until I was exposed to Github, a powerful tool that allows teams to execute effective project management. Its ability to document every change is essential to understand the progress of the project or assignment. 

<img class="ui small right floated image" src="https://askleo.askleomedia.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/cpu.jpg">

The ability to create and merge branches takes parallel working to another level. We can think of our team as a central processing unit (CPU). Of course, a human team is nowhere near a modern Intel or AMD CPU, but let's just pretend we are. Each member of a team is considered a single core in the entire CPU and each core is able to perform jobs in parallel. This means that each job does not overlap with one another and they will eventually be completed to fulfill the overall goal.

## Software Engineering

Out in the real world, no person is ever working alone. One day everyone will find themselves working in some kind of team whether they like it or not. With software engineering, it is essential to study effective techniques that will not only help yourself, but also benefit the entire team. When it comes to teamwork, communication and organization are the keys for success.
